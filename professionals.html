About the app

One shared place for the child: across homes

Many parents who share care lack a common structure for everyday cooperation. Information is scattered across texts, emails, and verbal agreements, and one parent ends up coordinating everything. Lina brings the practical details of medical information, contacts, equipment lists, photos, and messages into one shared space accessible in both homes.

Download: App Store · Google Play

When Lina is a good fit

Lina works well when parents

  • have a fundamentally workable co-parenting relationship but struggle with the practical coordination
  • want more structure and predictability in their everyday routines
  • need a shared communication channel that keeps cooperation focused
  • want to ensure important information about the child is available in both homes

Lina is not suitable when

  • there is high conflict requiring legal or therapeutic intervention
  • one or both parents are not ready for structured digital communication
  • the situation requires measures beyond practical coordination

How it works in practice

Structure that reduces friction in everyday life

Threads

Parents communicate about one topic at a time, whether logistics, school, or health, without important information getting lost in a long message thread.

Messages

Messages cannot be edited or deleted. This creates a predictable communication channel and a clear record of what has been agreed.

Shared album

Both parents can share photos from everyday life. The child is visible in both homes.

Child information

Medical information, allergies, and important details are entered once and available to both parents, regardless of which home the child is in.

Equipment lists

A clear overview of what the child needs in each home reduces the number of clarifications and misunderstandings around practical details.

Care Agreement

The care agreement page gives parents an overview of what the agreement should include, with an example and links to official resources. A good starting point before or after mediation.

View the information page

Thoughtful details

Designed with care for sensitive situations

Blur photos

Parents can blur individual photos in the shared album. This helps manage emotional reactions and keeps the focus on practical cooperation.

Blur messages

Parents can blur message content in a thread. The message is still there, but can be revealed when the reader is ready, reducing pressure in difficult conversations.

Mark important

Important messages can be marked and stay easy to find. Agreements, schedules, and key decisions are always accessible, not buried in a long thread.

Export conversations

All messages can be exported as a file. If a conversation needs to be shared with a mediator, solicitor, or other professional, parents have a clear record.
