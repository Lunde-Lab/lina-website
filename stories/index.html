Everyday scenarios

How parents use Lina

Small, everyday moments where having one shared place makes the difference. These are the situations Lina is built for.

Child information

The allergy the other home did not know about

A child is diagnosed with a new food allergy at a doctor's visit. The information needs to reach the other home before the next handover.

With Lina: The parent updates the child's medical information once. The other parent sees it immediately — no need for a separate text or call.

Equipment lists

The rain jacket that is always at the wrong house

A child arrives at school without rain gear because no one was sure which home had it. Small things like this create unnecessary friction.

With Lina: Shared equipment lists show what is at each home. The list stays up to date, so the jacket follows the child.

Threads

The school event buried in a text chain

School sends information about a parents' meeting. One parent forwards it by text, but the details get lost between practical messages about pickup and dinner.

With Lina: A dedicated thread for school keeps everything together. Details are easy to find without scrolling through unrelated messages.

Shared album

The first bicycle ride you almost missed

A child learns to ride a bicycle at one home. The other parent only hears about it days later and never sees the moment.

With Lina: Photos go into the shared album. Moments are shared automatically — regardless of which home the child is in.

Care schedule

The handover mix-up on a holiday week

Holiday weeks change the regular schedule. Both parents remember a different version of the plan, and the child ends up in the middle.

With Lina: The care schedule shows both parents the same overview. Changes are visible immediately, so there is one version of the truth.

Contacts

The dentist number no one could find

A child has a toothache at one home. The parent needs to call the dentist but does not have the number — it was saved in the other parent's phone.

With Lina: Shared contacts mean both parents have the dentist, doctor, and school numbers available at all times.

These are not dramatic problems. They are the quiet, everyday moments where a shared overview saves time, reduces stress, and keeps the focus on the child.
